Problems with the project

  • Lack of clarity
    • The project requirements are vague and/or my team is not sure as to what the project should achieve. Suggestions:
      • Review the section on project planning.
      • Familiarise yourself with the project by:
        • Looking at the project brief or talking to the client.
        • Researching other projects that may be similar to yours.
        • Drawing up and agreeing project objectives.
      • List aspects of the project that you find confusing and discuss these with your team and/or client.
    • After team meetings I can't quite recall what happened and/or I'm not sure what I am supposed to achieve by the next meeting. Suggestions:
      • Revisit the section on effective team meetings.
      • Ensure that you have a timeline or a Gantt chart and that it's available to all the team members.
      • Use an action list to make sure that any tasks to be carried out are written down. Assign responsibilities to these tasks and set a deadline for each. Make sure that all team members have access to this list.
      • Try using the Four-Step Project checklist to provide your meetings with more structure.
    • At team meetings my team doesn't really get much work done and there doesn't seem to be a sense of purpose. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that a meeting agenda is circulated prior to the meeting.
      • Ensure that you have a meeting chair and that they are aware of their responsibilities.
      • Revisit the section on effective team meetings.
      • Make use of a whiteboard in team discussions. This way all team members can get involved in the discussion process.
      • Make sure that a team member is taking minutes and that these are distributed after the meeting.
  • Lack of commitment
    • Team members were given tasks but no one seems to be doing anything. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that tasks and deadlines have been agreed and accepted by all team members
      • Call a progress meeting and address the issues as a team. Be prepared to revise allocations and timelines as necessary.
      • At the progress meeting, agree stages for completion in time for the next meeting. Ask team members to report back on their tasks at this meeting.
      • Revisit Possible Causes of conflict - Losing interest and Lencioni's 5 dysfunctions of teams.
    • I've sent out an important email/SMS/Facebook post but I've received no response. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that you have the correct contact details.
      • Try a different method of communication. For example, if you sent an email, try calling or following up with an SMS asking for a response.
      • At your next team meeting, address the issue and agree a time frame for responses to communication.
      • Revisit the section on effective communication.
  • Lack of ideas
    • My team does not appear to be very creative. Suggestions:
      • revisit the section on team decision making
      • employ a creativity technique https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creativity_techniques
    • My team doesn't explore alternatives and/or we get fixed on an idea without sufficient analysis. Suggestions:
      • Revisit the section on decision making and utilise the techniques.
      • When a decision point arises, ensure that the team acknowledges that there are options. Use a whiteboard in meetings to identify and explore these options.
      • Ensure that you are assertive in putting forward your ideas.
      • Ensure that your team members have a voice and are being listened to.
    • I feel that other team members are dismissive of my ideas and sometimes ignore me. Suggestions:
      • You and your team may need to work on assertiveness and cooperation.
      • Openly state your concerns in the next team meeting and ask for resolution. For example, ask for 'air time' and a considered discussion of your ideas before the team moves on.

Problems with Project Output/s

  • I'm not sure that my team will finish on time. Suggestions:
    • Ensure the team knows what is required and tasks are clear.
    • Construct a timeline or Gantt chart with objectives, tasks, and deadlines clearly defined.
    • Ensure that all tasks have been allocated.
    • Give someone the responsibility of directing the efforts of the team and ensuring that tasks are completed on time.
    • Plan regular team meetings to ensure progress is satisfactory.
  • We don't seem to have produced anything. Suggestions:
    • Revisit developing SMART goals.
    • Review your project schedule or Gantt Chart and ensure that you are on time.
    • Consider breaking down tasks into sub-tasks that will produce intermediate outputs that can be used as stage milestones.
    • Revisit Lencioni's 5 dysfunctions of teams.

Problems with team members

  • Interpersonal conflict
    • I feel that other team members are dismissive of my ideas and sometimes ignore me. Suggestions:
      • You and your team may need to work on assertiveness and cooperation.
      • Openly state your concerns in the next team meeting and ask for resolution. For example, ask for 'air time' and a considered discussion of your ideas before the team moves on.
      • Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict - People being actively critical.
    • Some team members never seem to complete their work on time and/or to a reasonable standard. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that they are aware of their tasks, expected standards, and associated timelines.
      • Openly discuss the issue at a team meeting. Details of how to do this can be found in Social Loafing.
      • Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict, as there are many possible causes (and brief solutions) here.
    • Some team members miss team meetings without a justifiable reason. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that your Team Charter clearly outlines expectations for team meeting attendance.
      • Ensure that they aware of the team meeting times - consider reminding them by email or SMS.
      • Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict as there are many possible causes (and brief solutions) here.
    • There is a lack of trust within my team and/or the team seems to be split into factions. Suggestions:
      • Ensure that management processes are transparent, all team members have access to all project documents, and communications are sent to everyone.
      • Revisit Lencioni's five dysfunctions of teams.
      • Ensure that the team meets frequently and that concerns are voiced so that the team can discuss openly. Practice engaged listening.
      • Revisit the section on conflict resolution.
      • Consider using a team facilitator if this situation continues.
    • My team is in a state of conflict. Suggestions:
      • Revisit the possible causes of the conflict and take the team conflict survey.
      • Call a team meeting specifically to address the issues and revisit the team charter and the team rules.
      • Give team members a chance to air their issues. Follow the procedure for conflict resolution.
      • Consider using a team facilitator.
  • Roles/leadership
    • My team has no leader. Suggestions:
      • You might not need a leader. Evaluate team performance and unless there are problems or dysfunctions (Lencioni's 5 dysfunctions of teams), there is nothing that you need to do.
      • If your team is not performing then a leader should be nominated/elected. Call a meeting to openly discuss the issues and decide a strategy for moving forward.
      • Consider taking the position yourself.
    • I don't feel that my team leader is up to the task. Suggestions:
      • Initially discuss your concerns directly with the team leader in a one-on-one meeting.
      • At this meeting offer to assist them as necessary.
      • If the situation does not change, call a team meeting and discuss the issues as a team.
      • Consider calling in a team facilitator.

      • Revisit the section on effective communication.
    • My team needs to prepare a report and we are having trouble achieving this. Suggestions:
      • Construct a template for your report complete with a table of contents.
      • Decide file naming conventions and document storage procedures.
      • Assign sections of the report to team members with agreed dates for completion.
      • Nominate a team member to amalgamate the sections.
      • Schedule a review session for the whole team where the report can be proofed and any changes agreed by the team.
      • Support team members for whom English may not be their first language as necessary.
