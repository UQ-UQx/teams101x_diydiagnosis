  • Problems with the project
    • Lack of clarity
      • The project requirements are vague and/or my team is not sure as to what the project should achieve.

        1. Review the section on project planning.
        2. Familiarise yourself with the project by:
          • Looking at the project brief or talking to the client.
          • Researching other projects that may be similar to yours.
          • Drawing up and agreeing project objectives.
        3. List aspects of the project that you find confusing and discuss these with your team and/or client.

      • After team meetings I can’t quite recall what happened and/or I’m not sure what I am supposed to achieve by the next meeting.

        1. Revisit the section on effective team meetings.
        2. Ensure that you have a timeline or a Gantt chart and that it’s available to all the team members.
        3. Use an action list to make sure that any tasks to be carried out are written down. Assign responsibilities to these tasks and set a deadline for each. Make sure that all team members have access to this list.
        4. Try using the Four-Step Project checklist to provide your meetings with more structure.

      • At team meetings my team doesn’t really get much work done and there doesn’t seem to be a sense of purpose.

        1. Ensure that a meeting agenda is circulated prior to the meeting.
        2. Ensure that you have a meeting chair and that they are aware of their responsibilities.
        3. Revisit the section on effective team meetings.
        4. Make use of a whiteboard in team discussions. This way all team members can get involved in the discussion process.
        5. Make sure that a team member is taking minutes and that these are distributed after the meeting.

    • Lack of commitment
      • Team members were given tasks but no one seems to be doing anything.

        1. Ensure that tasks and deadlines have been agreed and accepted by all team members
        2. Call a progress meeting and address the issues as a team. Be prepared to revise allocations and timelines as necessary.
        3. At the progress meeting, agree stages for completion in time for the next meeting. Ask team members to report back on their tasks at this meeting.
        4. Revisit Possible Causes of conflict – Losing interest and Lencioni’s 5 dysfunctions of teams.

      • I’ve sent out an important email/SMS/Facebook post but I’ve received no response.

        1. Ensure that you have the correct contact details.
        2. Try a different method of communication. For example, if you sent an email, try calling or following up with an SMS asking for a response.
        3. At your next team meeting, address the issue and agree a time frame for responses to communication.
        4. Revisit the section on effective communication.

    • Lack of ideas
      • My team does not appear to be very creative.

        1. revisit the section on team decision making
        2. employ a creativity technique https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creativity_techniques

      • My team doesn’t explore alternatives and/or we get fixed on an idea without sufficient analysis.

        1. Revisit the section on decision making and utilise the techniques.
        2. When a decision point arises, ensure that the team acknowledges that there are options. Use a whiteboard in meetings to identify and explore these options.
        3. Ensure that you are assertive in putting forward your ideas.
        4. Ensure that your team members have a voice and are being listened to.

      • I feel that other team members are dismissive of my ideas and sometimes ignore me.

        1. You and your team may need to work on assertiveness and cooperation.
        2. Openly state your concerns in the next team meeting and ask for resolution. For example, ask for ‘air time’ and a considered discussion of your ideas before the team moves on.

  • Lack of project output(s)
    • I’m not sure that my team will finish on time.

      1. Ensure the team knows what is required and tasks are clear.
      2. Construct a timeline or Gantt chart with objectives, tasks, and deadlines clearly defined.
      3. Ensure that all tasks have been allocated.
      4. Give someone the responsibility of directing the efforts of the team and ensuring that tasks are completed on time.
      5. Plan regular team meetings to ensure progress is satisfactory.

    • We don’t seem to have produced anything.

      1. Revisit developing SMART goals.
      2. Review your project schedule or Gantt Chart and ensure that you are on time.
      3. Consider breaking down tasks into sub-tasks that will produce intermediate outputs that can be used as stage milestones.
      4. Revisit Lencioni’s 5 dysfunctions of teams.

  • Problems with team members
    • Interpersonal conflict
      • I feel that other team members are dismissive of my ideas and sometimes ignore me.

        1. You and your team may need to work on assertiveness and cooperation.
        2. Openly state your concerns in the next team meeting and ask for resolution. For example, ask for ‘air time’ and a considered discussion of your ideas before the team moves on.
        3. Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict – People being actively critical.

      • Some team members never seem to complete their work on time and/or to a reasonable standard.

        1. Ensure that they are aware of their tasks, expected standards, and associated timelines.
        2. Openly discuss the issue at a team meeting. Details of how to do this can be found in Social Loafing.
        3. Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict, as there are many possible causes (and brief solutions) here.

      • Some team members miss team meetings without a justifiable reason.

        1. Ensure that your Team Charter clearly outlines expectations for team meeting attendance.
        2. Ensure that they aware of the team meeting times – consider reminding them by email or SMS.
        3. Revisit Possible Causes of Conflict as there are many possible causes (and brief solutions) here.

      • There is a lack of trust within my team and/or the team seems to be split into factions.

        1. Ensure that management processes are transparent, all team members have access to all project documents, and communications are sent to everyone.
        2. Revisit Lencioni’s five dysfunctions of teams.
        3. Ensure that the team meets frequently and that concerns are voiced so that the team can discuss openly. Practice engaged listening.
        4. Revisit the section on conflict resolution.
        5. Consider using a team facilitator if this situation continues.

      • My team is in a state of conflict.

        1. Revisit the possible causes of the conflict and take the team conflict survey.
        2. Call a team meeting specifically to address the issues and revisit the team charter and the team rules.
        3. Give team members a chance to air their issues. Follow the procedure for conflict resolution.
        4. Consider using a team facilitator.

    • Roles/leadership
      • My team has no leader.

        1. You might not need a leader. Evaluate team performance and unless there are problems or dysfunctions (Lencioni’s 5 dysfunctions of teams), there is nothing that you need to do.
        2. If your team is not performing then a leader should be nominated/elected. Call a meeting to openly discuss the issues and decide a strategy for moving forward.
        3. Consider taking the position yourself.

      • I don’t feel that my team leader is up to the task.

        1. Initially discuss your concerns directly with the team leader in a one-on-one meeting.
        2. At this meeting offer to assist them as necessary.
        3. If the situation does not change, call a team meeting and discuss the issues as a team.
        4. Consider calling in a team facilitator.

    • Communication

      • I’ve sent out an important email/SMS/Facebook post but I’ve received no response.

        1. Ensure that you have the correct contact details.
        2. Try a different method of communication. For example, if you sent an email, try calling or following up with an SMS asking for a response.
        3. At your next team meeting, address the issue and agree a time frame for responses to communication.
        4. Revisit the section on effective communication.

      • My team needs to prepare a report and we are having trouble achieving this.

        1. Construct a template for your report complete with a table of contents.
        2. Decide file naming conventions and document storage procedures.
        3. Assign sections of the report to team members with agreed dates for completion.
        4. Nominate a team member to amalgamate the sections.
        5. Schedule a review session for the whole team where the report can be proofed and any changes agreed by the team.
        6. Support team members for whom English may not be their first language as necessary.
